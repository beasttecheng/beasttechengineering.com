## SAMPLE COURSE SCRIPT — COURSE 1, MODULE 1

**Course:** AI Fundamentals for Licensed Professionals
**Module 1:** What AI Actually Is (and Isn't)
**Time:** ~20 minutes

---

**[INSTRUCTOR ON CAMERA — casual but professional setting]**

"If you've stamped a drawing, signed a tax return, or had your name on a legal brief, you've accepted professional liability. That's what a license means.

Now AI is in the room. And the question isn't whether it changes your work — it already has. The question is whether you understand it well enough to use it responsibly — or whether you're going to find out its limits the hard way, after a problem surfaces.

Let's fix that."

---

**[SLIDE: "AI Is Not a Magic Box"]**

"Most of the confusion around AI — and most of the liability exposure — comes from treating it like a magic box. Input goes in, answer comes out, trust it.

That's not how this works. And understanding *why* it's not how this works is the first thing that separates a professional who uses AI responsibly from one who doesn't."

---

**[SLIDE: "What an LLM Actually Does" — Simple Diagram]**

"A large language model — Claude, GPT, Gemini — is a statistical prediction engine. It was trained on an enormous amount of text. When you give it a prompt, it predicts, token by token, what text is most likely to come next, given its training.

That's it. That's the engine.

What makes modern LLMs useful is the scale of training, the quality of the feedback process used to align them to be helpful and accurate, and the increasingly sophisticated ways they can reason through complex problems.

But the fundamental mechanism — predicting likely text — is what explains both its power and its failure modes."

---

**[SLIDE: "Three Things AI Does Well"]**

"AI is genuinely excellent at three categories of tasks:

**One:** Processing and synthesizing large amounts of text quickly. Reading a 400-page specification and pulling out the relevant sections. That's real value.

**Two:** Drafting and structuring. Giving you a first draft of a report, a contract clause, a summary memo. You edit, you verify, you sign. But the blank page is no longer a problem.

**Three:** Explaining complex topics clearly. If you need to understand a regulatory framework you've never worked with, AI can walk you through it faster than any textbook.

Where it struggles: novel reasoning without precedent in its training data, anything requiring real-world verification, and tasks where being 95% right is worse than useless — because the 5% error is catastrophic."

---

**[SLIDE: "What This Means for Your License"]**

"Here's the professional reality: when you use AI to assist your work, you don't transfer liability to the AI. The PE stamp, the tax return signature, the brief with your bar number — those are still yours.

AI is a tool. A powerful one. But you are still the licensed professional of record.

So the question I want you to ask every time you use AI in your practice is: *'Do I understand this output well enough to put my name on it?'*

If the answer is yes — great. If the answer is no — you have more work to do before you sign anything."

---

**[TRANSITION]**

"In the next module, we'll go deeper into how language models are actually built — just enough mechanical understanding to help you predict when they'll fail you. Let's go."

## COHORT WORKSHOP EXERCISE — "BUILD YOUR FIRST PROFESSIONAL PROMPT"

**Exercise: 30 minutes**

**Setup:**
"You're going to build a prompt for a real task in your practice. By the end of this exercise, you'll have a reusable prompt template you can take back to your desk today."

**Step 1 (5 min):** Identify a repeatable, time-consuming task in your work that involves reading, summarizing, or drafting text.

**Step 2 (5 min):** Write a basic prompt for this task. Don't overthink it — just try it.

**Step 3 (10 min):** Apply the CREO framework:
- **C**ontext: Who are you? What's the situation?
- **R**ole: What role should Claude play?
- **E**xamples: Show it one example of a good output
- **O**utput format: Specify exactly what you want back

**Step 4 (5 min):** Test your CREO prompt in Claude. Compare the output to your Step 2 version.

**Step 5 (5 min):** Share your before/after with the group. What changed?

**Debrief:**
"What you just did is prompt engineering. It's not magic — it's structured communication. And it's now a professional skill."
