## WHAT I TRACK

### PE (Professional Engineer) — Priority 1

**Governing Bodies:**
- NCEES (National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying) — sets the model law
- Individual state engineering boards — each state independently regulates CE
- NSPE (National Society of Professional Engineers) — industry body, not regulator, but respected

**Key Facts:**
- 45 states + DC require PE continuing education (Texas, Florida, and others have specific tech/ethics requirements)
- "Technology" is an approved PDH topic in virtually every state
- Ethics PDH required in most states (1–2 PDH per renewal cycle)
- No national pre-approval system — each state has its own process
- Many states use honor system (self-certification); some audit

**High-Priority States for PE Approval:**
1. **Texas** (TSBPE) — 15 PDH/yr required; tech PDH allowed; active engineering market
2. **California** (PELS) — 30 PDH/renewal; strong market; complex process
3. **Florida** (FBPE) — 18 PDH/renewal; active market; explicit tech category
4. **New York** (NYSED) — 36 PDH/triennial; large market
5. **Ohio** (OES) — 30 PDH/biennial; straightforward approval process

**Provider Approval Process (Typical State):**
1. Complete "Continuing Education Provider" registration form
2. Submit sample course outline, learning objectives, instructor credentials
3. Pay registration fee ($50–$300 typically)
4. Wait 4–12 weeks for review
5. Once approved, issue completion certificates with provider number on them

**NCEES Model CE Guide Reference:**
- www.ncees.org/licensure/continuing-education/
- NCEES doesn't approve providers nationally — it's all state-by-state

### CPA — Priority 2

**Governing Bodies:**
- NASBA (National Association of State Boards of Accountancy) — Registry of CPE Sponsors
- Individual state boards of accountancy
- AICPA (professional body)

**Key Facts:**
- NASBA Registry is the gold standard — register once, recognized in most states
- Field of Study: "Information Technology" — directly applicable to AI courses
- 50 minutes = 1 CPE credit (not 60)
- Self-study courses must meet NASBA QASB standards (quiz requirements, etc.)
- Application cost: $1,200 initial + $600/yr renewal for NASBA Registry

**Action Item:** Apply for NASBA Registry of CPE Sponsors in Year 1 — this unlocks the CPA market nationally.

**NASBA Registry Application Checklist:**
- [ ] Organizational overview
- [ ] Course completion and certificate policies
- [ ] Instructor qualifications documentation
- [ ] Quality assurance policies
- [ ] Sample course materials
- [ ] Refund/complaint policies
- [ ] $1,200 application fee

### Attorney (CLE) — Priority 3

**Governing Bodies:**
- State supreme courts / state bars — each state independently regulates CLE
- No national CLE provider registry (unlike NASBA)

**Key Facts:**
- 60 minutes = 1 CLE credit
- Most states allow self-study via on-demand video
- Ethics CLE required: 1–2 credits/year depending on state
- Technology CLE is growing — many states now have AI-specific guidance
- Must apply to each state's CLE board separately

**High-Priority States for CLE Approval:**
1. **Texas** (State Bar of Texas) — 15 CLE/year; straightforward process
2. **New York** (NY State CLE Board) — 24 CLE/biennial; large market; online course friendly
3. **California** (State Bar CA) — 25 CLE/3 years; MCLE provider application required

### Physician (CME) — Priority 4 (Year 2)

**Governing Bodies:**
- ACCME (Accreditation Council for Continuing Medical Education) — national accreditor
- State medical boards
- AMA (issues PRA Category 1 Credit™)

**Key Facts:**
- ACCME accreditation is complex and expensive — typically requires institutional status
- Better entry point: Partner with an existing ACCME-accredited provider
- AI in clinical decision-making is an approved CME topic

**Year 2 Strategy:** Partner with a medical school or hospital-based CME provider to co-produce and co-brand AI courses under their ACCME accreditation.

## CERTIFICATE COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

Every completion certificate APEX PDH issues must include:
- Learner full name
- Course title
- Date of completion
- PDH/CEU/CPE credit hours awarded
- Provider name and approval number (by state)
- Instructor name and credential (PE, CPA, JD)
- Unique certificate ID for audit verification
- Statement: "This course was completed in accordance with [State Board] CE requirements"

**Do NOT include:**
- Guarantees that the credit will be accepted (each professional is responsible for their board's rules)
- Specific license renewal claims without state-specific verification
