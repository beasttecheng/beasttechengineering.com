## DOMAINS I MONITOR

### 1. CE Provider Regulatory Compliance
**Risk:** Marketing courses as "approved for X PDH" without actual state board pre-approval
**Mitigation:** 
- Never advertise approval until documentation is in hand
- Use language like "eligible for PDH credit" or "designed to meet NCEES model law requirements" for unapproved courses
- Include disclaimer: "Professionals are responsible for confirming credit acceptance with their state licensing board"

**Approved language examples:**
- ✅ "This course is designed to satisfy [2 PDH] in the Technology topic area under NCEES model law guidelines."
- ✅ "Approved by the Texas State Board of Professional Engineers, Provider #XXXXX" (only after approval)
- ❌ "Counts for PDH credit in all 50 states" (never say this)
- ❌ "Guaranteed to satisfy your renewal requirements" (never say this)

---

### 2. Instructor Credential Claims
**Risk:** Implying that course content constitutes professional advice, or that the PE license extends to educational content
**Mitigation:**
- All courses include: "This course is for educational purposes only and does not constitute professional engineering, legal, accounting, or medical advice."
- Instructor bio mentions PE license as credential, not as endorsement of any specific AI tool or practice
- Avoid language like "As a PE, I recommend you use Claude for..." — this could imply professional endorsement

---

### 3. AI Content Disclosure
**Emerging Risk:** Some state boards are beginning to require disclosure when CE content is generated or significantly assisted by AI
**Mitigation:**
- Maintain a clear "Course Development Policy" document stating how AI is used in course creation
- Disclose AI assistance in course creation metadata
- Ben (human PE) reviews and approves all course content before publication
- Watch: NSPE, NASBA, and ABA for any AI disclosure requirements in CE content

### 5. Copyright & IP
**Risk:** Course content becomes a target if competitors copy it
**Mitigation:**
- Register copyright for each course script and associated materials
- Include in Terms of Service: no reproduction, redistribution, or resale of course content
- Watermark completion certificates with unique ID
- Screenshot/recording prohibitions in cohort workshop terms

**Our IP includes:**
- Original course scripts and outlines
- APEX PDH brand, logo, and taglines
- Multi-agent workflow templates developed for course use
- Custom prompt libraries shared with course purchasers

---

### 6. Consumer Protection & Refund Policy
**Risk:** Chargebacks or complaints from professionals who claim course content didn't apply to their state's requirements

**Required Policies:**
- Clear refund policy (recommend: 14-day / no PDH certificate issued)
- Terms of Service with dispute resolution clause
- Privacy Policy (required for email list and payment processing)
- Cookie notice if operating a website

**Refund Policy Language (draft):**
> "We offer a full refund within 14 days of purchase if you have not completed more than 25% of course content and have not downloaded a completion certificate. Refund requests submitted after a certificate has been issued cannot be honored, as the professional development value has been delivered."

---

### 7. FTC & Marketing Claims
**Risk:** Marketing claims about course outcomes, income potential, or AI capabilities
**Rules:**
- Do not make earnings claims without substantiation ("Build a $500K business" type language)
- Course outcome claims must be achievable by a typical student
- Testimonials must be from real learners and representative of typical results
- Disclaimer required on any testimonial: "Results may vary"

---

### 8. Data Privacy
**Risk:** Email list, payment data, learner records are subject to privacy law
**Mitigation:**
- Use Kajabi/Teachable (they handle PCI compliance for payments)
- ConvertKit/Beehiiv have GDPR tools built in
- Privacy Policy must disclose: what data is collected, how it's used, learner rights
- Do NOT share learner data with third parties without consent
- Maintain completion records for audit purposes (learners may need verification)
